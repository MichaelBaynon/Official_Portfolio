Personal Bio

    My code journey started with just a genuine interest in computers and how they work. When I realized software development was something I could do I never looked back. I did not want to jump blind in so I did a lot of research like I mean A LOT.

    My initial plan was to be self taught. That worked for a while and I learned a lot, working on my own was no problem. Then I realized there is almost no way someone is not teaching this and my time could be better spent just learning instead of having to figure out what to learn.

    At this point I found a coding bootcamp called Lambda School. Within a few days I was blown away with their track record and signed up. I enrolled in the Full Stack Web Development course and let me tell you. Never have I ever learned so much so fast. I spent 5 days a week for months learning code, talking about code, and essentially becoming a web developer.

    Im glad I found Lambda. If it wasnt for Lambda I may have never became a Full Stack Developer. Lambda taught me how to work in team and communicate. They taught me version control and how to learn from others. Throughout my Lambda journey I worked in my groups both small and big. I also had a daily standup of what I achieved that day and what we were to expect for the rest of the day. I beleive this has prepared me for just about any workplace.
